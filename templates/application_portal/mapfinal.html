Data Section

Land administration provides documentation on people to the land relationships. Datasets serve as the key requirement for any decision making tool or in any GIS.The pilot LADM adopted the model-driven approach.The land parcel data serves as the primary section in any land administsration system and so was the case in this system.

The datasets used included;


  • Land Parcels- Served as the basic unit in the land administration section.
  • Landuse data- This was used to place constraints in the system on actions on land parcels and also guide on the landuse zoning of the study area.
  • Landcover data- Used to guide on the issues of change of user in the system
  • Rivers data- Used to generate the riparian reserves
  • Riparian- To guide on the areas that need attention in land management to avoid depletion of important natural resources (alongside rivers)

More data is being considered so as to help in better decision making on matter pertaining land administration.

LADM Project

Overview

The land management policy in a country is critical component to climate change adaptation because it helps in establishing and mainstreaming the necessary conditions for policies and actions for climate change mitigation. Currently, Kenyaâ€™s land policy lacks linkage with climate change adaptation and mitigation. There is pressing need to incorporate concerns about climate change and natural disaster to land policy and governance in Kenya.

Project Objectives

DeKut aims to implement a prototype land administration system that draws upon evolving practices in sustainable land management and land governance along with information and communication technology and geographic information systems. Building sustainable and spatially enabled land administration will help document land use and point out those that are under unsustainable use. This project therefore, will act as a starting point in the step towards establishing a linkage between land management and climate change adaptation and mitigation.
